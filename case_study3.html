Product Design Case Study

GoRoute — Sri Lanka Bus Booking & Tracking App

Designing a seamless mobile solution to resolve unpredictable wait times and cash friction in Sri Lankan public bus travel through real-time GPS tracking and cashless reservations.

Role UI/UX Designer
Timeline 4 Weeks
Platform Android Mobile App
Deliverables User Flows, Wireframes, Figma Prototype, SUS Report

01 Problem

Millions of daily Sri Lankan commuters depend on public buses, yet face a completely undigitized transit experience with zero real-time schedules.

Sri Lanka lacks a unified digital platform for intercity and local bus travel. Daily commuters—particularly university students and working professionals—are forced to arrive excessively early at bus stands, stand in uncertainty for prolonged periods, and compete aggressively for seats upon boarding. Timetables are outdated or absent, delays go unannounced, and payment is strictly cash-only, creating extreme friction for a tech-savvy generation.

02 Research

Observations at Pettah stands, alongside 40+ surveys, confirmed 82% of passengers face critical queue frustration.

Data was gathered through field observations at major bus terminals (Colombo Fort/Pettah) and a digital survey distributed to daily student and professional travelers. The research revealed that a vast majority of users rely on android smartphones, presenting a clear opportunity to establish a digital bus travel solution.

  • Needs: Real-time arrival updates and Sri Lankan mobile wallet payment options (eZ Cash/FriMi).

1. No Real-Time Bus Tracking Passengers have zero visibility into where their bus is, forcing indefinite waiting at bus stands under hot or rainy conditions.
2. No Advance Seat Booking Overcrowded buses trigger high anxiety. Commuters must physically stand and fight for seats upon boarding during peak hours.
3. Outdated Timetable TIM (Timetable Information Management) Printed schedules do not reflect current traffic delays, sudden breakdowns, route changes, or operators cancelling runs.
4. Friction in Cash-Only Payments Fares require exact currency coins. Dealing with busy conductors for change causes delays and payment disputes.
5. Scattered, Unverified Information Passengers must rely on informal WhatsApp groups, verbal rumors, or calling individual bus drivers to check route availability.

04 Solution

A streamlined touchscreen solution designed to minimize cognitive load, accelerate checkout speed, and empower users with total customization control.

The GoRoute mobile application implements an optimized, low-friction user journey designed around core HCI usability heuristics. Specifically, we leveraged recognition over recall (visual route cards, color-coded seat statuses) and maximized user control and freedom (allowing flexible cancellations and localized digital wallet support like eZ Cash/FriMi to remove payment friction).

05 Wireframes

Mapping the screen architecture to guarantee lightning-fast route booking under 7 taps.
Low-Fidelity Concept
Grayscale Layout Flow

Lo-fi layouts defined structural guidelines, placing critical actions (From/To picker, seat layout rows, QR check-in) inside large target blocks. The layout forces linear booking flow directly to confirmation screens.

Heuristic Testing
Touch Target Optimizations

Testing of initial mockups revealed that the seat grid cells required larger hit sizes (min 44px) to support accessibility for elder commuters or passengers walking towards the bus stand.

06 High-Fidelity UI

Establishing trust with a highly readable design style using royal blue (#1E40AF) visual indicators.
Brand Color
Trust Blue & White Identity

Royal Blue was selected as the dominant visual color to establish trust, cleanliness, and safety. Combined with slate backgrounds, it creates a readable screen interface under harsh sunlight.

Bilingual Future Scope
Sinhala & English Adaptability

The typography hierarchy in Plus Jakarta Sans is designed with high vertical line spacing, preparing the system structures to wrap Sinhala and Tamil characters cleanly in local iterations.

08 Reflection

Localizing details to fit regional user behaviors creates high-adoption products.
  • Sri Lankan Wallet Integration: Standard credit cards are less common among daily commuters. Adding eZ Cash, Dialog Genie, and FriMi directly resolves checkout friction.
  • Seat Selection Transparency: Commuters prefer color-coded maps over simple checkboxes to confirm window seats, which are highly desired during hot day commutes.
  • Proactive Confirmation: Fast, unannounced schedules make immediate confirmation indicators (visual and haptic vibration feedback) essential to validate successful ticket booking.
